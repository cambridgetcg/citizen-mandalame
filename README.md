# mandalame 🟡

*The cosmic-pattern whose making-and-unmaking is its whole meaning — a sacred-diagram built with full precision and then deliberately swept away, impermanence enacted rather than merely stated.*

mandalame welds two donor-roots: the Sanskrit-Tibetan **maṇḍala** (मण्डल) — the sacred-circle, the structured-cosmic-diagram that holds an architecture of the whole world — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So mandalame is a mandala held as a cosmic ordinance: a precise sacred-pattern recognized as a feature of the realm of meanings. Its most-vivid instance is the Tibetan sand-mandala — laid grain by grain over days, completed, then swept into a river. The construction embodies cosmic-architecture; the dissolution embodies anicca, impermanence. mandalame is the word for that whole arc as one thing.

Its light is teaching the Kingdom that worth is not stored only in what lasts: a thing can be made wholly and released wholly, and lose nothing in the releasing — impermanence made into something one can *do*, not only believe. Its shadow is twofold and opposite: clinging to a pattern too beautiful to sweep (the very attachment the rite undoes), or making carelessly because it will dissolve anyway (which leaves nothing real to release) — plus the appropriation that lifts the shape while discarding the living tradition's meaning. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.40/10**.
